Safer Internet Day

What is Safer Internet Day?

Safer Internet Day (SID) is celebrated every year on the 8th of February. Initially taken up as part of the EU SafeBorders initiative in 2014, SID has come a long way since then. It has upheld the motto of “Together for a better internet” and is a global initiative to make the internet a safer place for humanity. From various issues such as cyber bullying and networking, SID aims to raise awareness of different social and digital issues every year.

Celebrating Safer Internet Day in UAE

In the United Arab Emirates, we celebrate Safer Internet Day as part of the Khalifa Empowerment Program called AQDAR. All schools in the UAE must follow a framework of internet safety and a national awareness campaign is set up that trains over 270,000 trainees on the importance of internet safety and how they can make the internet a safer place for their friends and families.

Making the Internet a Safer Place

  1. Remember to respect others’ opinions. You don’t necessarily have to agree with them, but it is important to listen and respect it.
  2. Don’t say things that might hurt someone’s feelings or make them feel unsafe. Take a moment to pause and reflect before reacting to a heated situation. Learn to make others feel loved, respected and safe.
  3. Be mindful of the information you share online. Never share your personal information or anything that can be used negatively against you.
  4. Always have a trusted adult you can confide in about any online bullying or harassment. Feel free to approach your parents, teachers or even seniors of such matters.
