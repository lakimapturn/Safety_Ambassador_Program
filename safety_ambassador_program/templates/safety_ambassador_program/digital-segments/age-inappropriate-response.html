The Internet is a big space. It is very open and vast, connecting people all around the world. It teaches us, students, so many important things. It is, also, very dangerous if we do not understand where we need to go or how we need to responsibly use the World Wide Web.

When you find yourself in a difficult situation, such as finding uncomfortable things online, you may be confused. You mustn't worry! The Safety Ambassadors encourage you to think of an adult- it may be your parents or your teachers. Letting an adult know about what you find disturbing is a good start to making the Internet a much safer space for you and your friends to learn and grow.

{% else %}

Seeing inappropriate content at a young age can leave children feeling confused and unable to process what they have seen or experienced.

We must first understand the effect it has on us…

Viewing age-inappropriate content online can subconsciously alter a student's developing mindset and encourage them to behave erratically. Inappropriate content hazards a safety risk- it can cause mental and emotional damage to children of any age, especially very young children. It can cause them to have nightmares or a change in behavior, more so if the content was very vivid.

Now that we've understood what is classified as inappropriate on the internet, we must ask ourselves:
What do we do when we find such content?

Stumbling upon inappropriate content at a young age can leave you feeling confused and unable to process what you have seen or experienced.

Reporting such a problem is a good step to take to protect your friends and yourself from the same problem. Alerting your teacher, your parent, a trusted adult or a Safety Ambassador is a powerful step to decrease your exposure to the same. We would be able to help you feel more comfortable online, safely browse your interests and learn new things!

Summarizing the points, we encourage you to do the following in the case that you have experienced uneasiness online, via. Content:

  1. Report the content/site
  2. Spread awareness of this problem by cautioning your peers.
  3. Alert/Inform a trusted teacher, parent or Safety Ambassador.
