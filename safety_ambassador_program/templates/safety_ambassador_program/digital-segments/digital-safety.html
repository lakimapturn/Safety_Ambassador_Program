Digital Safety

  1. Do not give your password, name, address, school name, or any other personal information about yourself or your family, to anyone, no matter how genuine the person might be. You are entitled to keep this important information to yourself – It is your own secret.
  2. On the internet, avoid chatting with strangers, no matter how safe the other person makes you feel. It could be a trap.
  3. If a profile asks for your name and address, don't fill it out. Check the authenticity of the form. It would be better to run it by an adult.
  4. Without the approval of an adult / parent, do not enter a chat room.
  5. If a profile asks for your name and address, don't fill it out.
  6. If you see something you believe your parents won't like, don't stay on that page/link and move to another.
  7. If you don't have your parents' consent, don't upload images of yourself online.
  8. Ask your parents, guardian or any trusted adult, if you have any questions regarding something you've read. You are not alone and the sooner you reach out with a concern, the better chances of resolving it before it gets worse.
