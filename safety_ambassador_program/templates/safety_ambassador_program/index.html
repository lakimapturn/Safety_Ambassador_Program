Digital Use

Digital Wellbeing is an important part of our lives. All people, irrespective of age, culture or colour, use modern technology and depend on it to complete daily tasks.

Take yourself as an example… you are accessing this wonderful platform of information through the help of your device and our best friend: The Internet. Sometimes, though, the Internet can be a confusing friend to deal with. Sometimes you spend so much time on the internet- on your device- that you forget about your real friends.

It is important to note the balance, and use the internet in a safe and healthy way.

Through this video we may be able to understand that balancing your digital life and your real life is very important. Further into the module we will explain the precautions you must keep in mind while using your devices.

Introducing Digital Wellbeing Experiments

Digital Identity

Digital Identity is a set of characteristics- a digital profile of your own personality- that often helps the internet identify you as a unique individual. It consists of your name, address, age, profession, email and more. It increases it’s knowledge on you with every click of your keyboard, every website you visit, and every item you shop online.

  1. Nothing is temporary online. Everything you do is recorded and monitored which is why you must be careful and aware of what you do on the internet.
  2. Keep your profile as private as possible. This makes it harder for hackers and stalkers to know about your life.
  3. Be aware of what you post online. Think twice about posting a picture that’s private to you. If there are others in the photo, make sure you have their consent before uploading it anywhere.
  4. It is important to stay calm while on the internet. Take breathers and breaks from social media. Avoid getting into any arguments or spats online.

Digital Safety

  1. Give no one your password, name, address, school name, or any other personal information about yourself or your family.
  2. On the internet, avoid chatting with strangers.
  3. Don't agree to meet anyone you've met online in person.
  4. If a profile asks for your name and address, don't fill it out.
  5. If you see something you believe your parents won't like, don't stay online.
  6. If you don't have your parents' consent, don't upload images of yourself online.
  7. Ask your parent or guardian if you have any questions regarding something you've read.

5 Internet Safety Tips for Kids

Digital Safety

  1. Ensure you have a strong password. Make sure it has at least one capital letter, a number and a special symbol.
  2. Keep your personal information to yourself. If you need to upload it somewhere, make sure you do so with your parents’ consent.
  3. Don’t immediately trust everything you see on the internet. You never know who is sitting behind the screen, thousands of miles away. It is always recommended to talk to your parents, teachers or elders about any suspicious activity that comes up.
  4. Avoid giving personal information such as your address, mobile number and age, to websites.
